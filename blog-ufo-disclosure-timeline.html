UFO Disclosure Timeline: Everything We Know in 2026

Published February 20, 2026 | 15 min read | By SpunkArt
UFO disclosure timeline UAP disclosure 2026 alien disclosure congressional hearings Pentagon UAP

The question of whether we are alone in the universe has shifted from the fringes of conspiracy theory to the center of mainstream politics and national security debate. In the span of just a few years, the United States government has gone from denying the existence of UFO programs to admitting it cannot explain hundreds of documented encounters with unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP). This timeline tracks every major milestone on the road to disclosure, from early government programs to the latest Congressional actions in 2026.

Whether you believe we are on the verge of confirming non-human intelligence or witnessing the greatest psyop in history, the facts documented below are matters of public record. Every event listed has been verified through official government documents, Congressional testimony, or credible journalistic reporting.

The Early Years: Government UFO Programs

1947

Roswell Incident & Project Sign

An unidentified object crashes near Roswell, New Mexico. The Army initially reports recovering a "flying disc" before changing the story to a weather balloon. The Air Force launches Project Sign, its first official UFO investigation program. Internal memos reveal some investigators conclude the objects are interplanetary.

1952 - 1969

Project Blue Book

The Air Force investigates 12,618 UFO sightings over 17 years. 701 cases remain officially "unidentified" when the project closes. The Condon Report recommends ending government UFO research, but many scientists who participated publicly disagree with the report's conclusions.

2007

AATIP Program Begins

Senator Harry Reid secures $22 million in black budget funding for the Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program (AATIP), run out of the Pentagon by intelligence officer Luis Elizondo. The program investigates military encounters with UAP and produces classified reports on advanced propulsion physics.

The Modern Disclosure Era (2017-2023)

December 2017

The New York Times Breaks the Story

The New York Times publishes "Glowing Auras and Black Money," revealing the existence of AATIP and releasing three Navy UAP videos (FLIR1, Gimbal, GoFast). Luis Elizondo goes public after resigning from the Pentagon, stating he left because the military was not taking the UAP issue seriously enough. This article is the inflection point that moves UFOs from conspiracy to mainstream.

April 2020

Pentagon Officially Releases UAP Videos

The Department of Defense officially releases the three Navy videos previously leaked in 2017, confirming their authenticity. The Pentagon states the videos show "unidentified aerial phenomena" and that releasing them "does not reveal any sensitive capabilities or systems." This is the first time the U.S. government officially acknowledges genuine UAP footage.

June 2021

DNI Preliminary Assessment

The Office of the Director of National Intelligence releases a landmark preliminary assessment on UAP. Key findings: 144 reports from military sources between 2004-2021. Only one case was identified (a deflating balloon). 18 incidents exhibited "unusual movement patterns or flight characteristics" including remaining stationary in high winds, moving against the wind, and making abrupt maneuvers without apparent propulsion. The report acknowledges UAP as a national security concern.

May 2022

First Congressional Hearing in 50 Years

The House Intelligence Subcommittee holds the first public Congressional hearing on UFOs since 1970. Deputy Director of Naval Intelligence Scott Bray testifies and shows previously classified UAP footage. Under Secretary of Defense Ronald Moultrie acknowledges over 400 UAP reports. The hearing reveals the creation of the new Airborne Object Identification and Management Synchronization Group (AOIMSG), later renamed AARO.

December 2022

NDAA 2023 and the Schumer Amendment

The National Defense Authorization Act for FY2023 includes provisions requiring the Department of Defense to establish a secure reporting mechanism for UAP encounters. The legislation also mandates that AARO conduct a historical review of all UAP-related government programs going back to 1945. Senator Kirsten Gillibrand champions the UAP provisions.

June 2023

David Grusch Goes Public

Former intelligence officer David Grusch, who served on the UAP Task Force, files a whistleblower complaint and goes public with extraordinary claims: the U.S. government has been running a crash retrieval and reverse-engineering program for non-human craft for decades. He states he was denied access to these programs and that colleagues who briefed him faced retaliation. The Intelligence Community Inspector General finds his complaint "credible and urgent."

July 2023

Historic Congressional Testimony

David Grusch, Commander David Fravor (Tic Tac encounter witness), and Ryan Graves (fighter pilot and UAP advocate) testify before the House Oversight Committee. Grusch testifies under oath that the government possesses "non-human biologics" recovered from crash sites. Fravor details his 2004 encounter with the Tic Tac object that defied known physics. Graves describes near-daily UAP encounters by Navy pilots. Both parties express bipartisan support for transparency.

December 2023

UAP Disclosure Act (Modified)

Senate Majority Leader Chuck Schumer introduces the UAP Disclosure Act modeled on the JFK Assassination Records Collection Act. The original bill would create an independent review board with eminent domain authority over UAP-related materials held by private contractors. The bill passes the Senate but is significantly weakened in the House, with key provisions stripped by Republican leadership, reportedly under pressure from defense contractors. A diluted version passes in the final NDAA.

2024: Acceleration

March 2024

AARO Historical Report Volume 1

The All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office releases Volume 1 of its historical review. The report concludes AARO has found "no verifiable evidence" that the U.S. government or private industry has ever possessed or reverse-engineered extraterrestrial technology. The report is widely criticized by members of Congress, whistleblowers, and UAP researchers who argue AARO was not given proper access to all relevant programs. Senator Gillibrand calls the report "incomplete."

June 2024

Immaculate Constellation

Journalist Michael Shellenberger reports on a classified UAP program called "Immaculate Constellation," allegedly a top-secret program within the intelligence community that has collected and analyzed UAP data for years, separate from AARO. Multiple sources corroborate the program's existence. The name itself is later confirmed by the DoD as a "controlled unclassified" term, adding credibility to the reporting.

November 2024

New Jersey Drone Wave

Beginning in late November, residents across New Jersey, New York, and the northeast United States report hundreds of sightings of large, unidentified drone-like objects. The objects are described as car-sized, silent or low-humming, with unusual light configurations. Military and civilian authorities cannot or will not identify the operators. The episode reignites public interest in UAP and raises serious questions about airspace security.

2025: The Pressure Builds

January - March 2025

New Administration, New Approach

The incoming administration signals increased interest in UAP transparency, driven partly by public pressure from the drone wave incidents. Multiple Cabinet-level officials make statements acknowledging the legitimacy of UAP concerns. The new Director of National Intelligence orders a comprehensive review of all UAP-related intelligence holdings.

June 2025

Senate Intelligence Committee Closed Briefings

Multiple rounds of classified briefings are held for members of the Senate Intelligence and Armed Services Committees. Sources indicate that new evidence has been presented that goes significantly beyond what was publicly available. Several senators emerge from briefings making stronger public statements than ever before about the need for transparency and the reality of unexplained phenomena.

September 2025

NDAA FY2026 UAP Provisions

The FY2026 National Defense Authorization Act includes the strongest UAP provisions to date. The legislation mandates declassification timelines for UAP-related materials, provides expanded whistleblower protections specifically for UAP witnesses, and creates a congressional oversight mechanism with access to classified UAP programs. Key provisions that were stripped in 2023 are restored in strengthened form.

2026: Where We Stand Now

January 2026

First Declassification Deadline

The first batch of materials mandated for declassification review under the FY2026 NDAA provisions reaches its deadline. While the full scope of released materials is still being analyzed, initial releases include previously classified sensor data, pilot reports, and analytical assessments that confirm objects exhibiting trans-medium travel (air to water), hypersonic velocities without visible propulsion, and anti-gravity characteristics.

February 2026

Where We Are Today

As of this writing, the disclosure process continues to accelerate. Key developments to watch: additional declassification releases scheduled for Q2 2026, the upcoming public hearings announced by the Senate Intelligence Committee, and the establishment of the independent review board mandated by the latest legislation. The scientific community is increasingly engaging with UAP data, with peer-reviewed papers appearing in mainstream journals.

The disclosure movement would not exist without individuals willing to risk their careers and reputations to speak publicly. Here are the most significant voices:

  • Commander David Fravor - Navy pilot who encountered the Tic Tac UAP off the coast of San Diego in 2004. His testimony is considered the gold standard for military UAP encounters.
  • Ryan Graves - Former Navy F/A-18 pilot who reported near-daily UAP encounters during training operations. Founded Americans for Safe Aerospace to advocate for pilot reporting.
  • Luis Elizondo - Former director of AATIP who resigned from the Pentagon in 2017, sparking the modern disclosure era. Author of "Imminent."
  • Karl Nell - Retired Army Colonel who publicly stated at the Sol Foundation conference that "non-human intelligence exists" and "UAP are real." His background includes senior positions in intelligence and aerospace.
  • Tim Gallaudet - Retired Rear Admiral who has spoken publicly about UAP encounters by Navy personnel and the institutional resistance to investigation.

The Evidence Categories

The UAP evidence available in 2026 falls into several categories of increasing significance:

Sensor Data

Multiple sensor modalities have captured UAP: infrared cameras (FLIR), radar (ship and aircraft), satellite imagery, electromagnetic spectrum sensors, and sonar (for trans-medium cases). The most compelling cases involve simultaneous detection across multiple independent sensor systems, ruling out equipment malfunction or misidentification.

Physical Evidence

Claims of physical materials from UAP encounters, including debris from alleged crash retrievals. Analysis by materials scientists has reportedly identified isotopic ratios not consistent with known terrestrial manufacturing. Congressional investigators are actively seeking access to these materials.

Witness Testimony

Hundreds of military witnesses, including pilots, radar operators, and intelligence analysts, have provided testimony through official channels. The credibility of these witnesses is established by their security clearances, professional records, and willingness to testify under oath.

Historical Documentation

Newly declassified documents reveal that government interest in UAP has been continuous since at least the late 1940s, contradicting decades of official denials. Programs with various names and classifications have operated across multiple agencies and administrations.
